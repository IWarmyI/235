Princess Match!

Darvin Muralitharan

Proposal

High Concept

You are a knight out to slay the dragon and save the princess. Race to match all cards as fast as possible to save the princess before time runs out!

Genre and Platform

Genre: Card

Platform: Desktop Web

Story

The King's daughter has been kidnapped and he appoints he top knight, you, to go out and rescue her.

Graphics

In terms of graphics, I'm aiming for a cartoony feel and definitely a light look on everything. There will be a timer and score counter left on the sides of the screen for the player to get information about their progress as well as a high score that will be kept locally.

Gameplay

The user will be able to select cards with their mouse and will learn from just clicking around and discovered the ability to select cards. After the user finishes all cards on screen and new set will show with more cards creating a new level. There will be 3 levels with the user gaining points for correct matches and losing points for incorrect matches.

To advance a level, an AI enemy will be shown with a set amount of health that the player must defeat or have more points than once all cards are gone. The AI will have the same scoring applied and can defeat you if it makes half of the available matches.

Design Process Notes

This project I had a very small idea of what I wanted to do. The original plan for my game had a much larger scope where a timer was included, and the player was able to take damage. However, due to time constraints, I ended up ultimately removing the timer and player damage. In the end the flow of my game and its pacing seemed to work pretty well still since instead of creating an endgame where the player would be defeated, the player could still go through all 3 levels just with a less possible score. My idea became one where a missed match gave time for the enemy to heal the worth of one attack in that level, this forced the player to actually pick cards carefully if they wanted to defeat the enemy for a bonus 5 points. The sounds were the last thing I implemented partly because they're easy and partly because I completely forgot about that requirement until I noticed someone ask a question about on slack. I definitely wish I could have done more and am considering maybe doing more with it after final grades have been posted and I'm waiting until the next semester, which would definitely help improve my portfolio.

Source Information

To shuffle the level deck and full deck, I used an array shuffle method I had found on Stack Overflow which allowed the deck to be shuffled with more variety in the card choices, though I do feel there is still some repetition. The hero, enemy, and card images were all used from the provided example. I probably should have asked about whether this was allowed or not but during the project, I used it as is because my art skills are not very good. The background image used to create a fantasy feeling was taken from Vector Stock. For sound effects, I found a nice card flip sound at Orange Free Sounds. The heal and sword swing sounds were taken from YouTube and converted to mp3. I did have to edit the sword effect so there wasn't a delay and the card flip effect so that the sound ended sooner.
